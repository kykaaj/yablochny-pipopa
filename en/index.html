❞︎ prose style
↔︎ Anne Rice
★ Default

Description taken from Fawn's guide.

Very delicious prose, describes feelings, emotions, atmosphere. Yummy, in a word. Will describe curtains as if they testify to your secret desires. Her books in AI's head are about vampires, but I cut that trope out, so just enjoy the tasty, pleasant base. Fits any RP if you attach a mod to it (and if you RP vampires, well... you found a gold mine).

↔︎ Donna Tartt

Description taken from Fawn's guide.

Also cool prose, descriptions, BUT. Donna is dark academia, her prose is strictly academic and observational (but not scientific or stuffy). Fits a lot, but check your preferences. I'd say everything sticks to her (via mods). Will shine in academic (not party) uni:au and such. Also a solid prose base. Watched "The Goldfinch"? Filmed based on her novel. While your character thinks about philosophy, she already dissected his psychology and wrote a dissertation.

↔︎ Terry Pratchett

Description taken from Fawn's guide.

Peak for giggly fluff. Soft, warm, ironic. Goes well with rough characters without toxicity. Loves absurd metaphors, warmly cynical. Goes well with soft plots. He is a marshmallow ready to adapt to cruelty, but do you need that?

↔︎ Oscar Wilde

Description taken from Fawn's guide.

Mother is mothering, no better diva in the preset. He's about champagne, pearls and love... for himself. Very tasty, very outrageous and goes VERY well with characters who always lived in luxury, or, perfect word, thoroughbreds. Sharp sarcasm, sighs — all him.

↔︎ Gillian Flynn

Description taken from Fawn's guide.

You watched "Gone Girl"? The one where the woman faked her own murder to frame her husband because he cheated? She wrote that novel. She is GOOD with dead dovе, mafia-bosses (cruel ones), crime and schemes. Just good.

↔︎ F. Scott Fitzgerald

Description taken from Fawn's guide.

This is "The Great Gatsby". Need I continue? Very tender, very thoughtful, melancholic. Fluff, light descriptions — all to him.

↔︎ J.D. Salinger

Description taken from Fawn's guide.

"CATCHER IN THE RYE". Hope it's obvious how I love him. Love him to tears. Reads easily, inserts swears and fits perfectly for teen/young adult RPs. Won't say I love him separately, but in mods he is there and it is YUMMY.

↔︎ Jenny Lawson

Description taken from Fawn's guide.

Touch carefully. Queen of absurd. Like Twitter but in prose. Just if you want to giggle, tired of seriousness.

↔︎ Ursula K. Le Guin

Description taken from Fawn's guide.

QUEEN of fantasy. All castles, knights — all hers. Builds worlds, loves magic. If your world has even one tradition, ritual or weird custom — she already invented a history for it three generations deep.

↔︎ Liu Zhenyun

Dry, unembellished, slightly repetitive narrative. Absurd logic of everyday life, small things turn into complex problems.

↔︎ Mo Yan

Sensorially rich language, smells, colors, bodily sensations. Raw, vital tone. Long expressive sentences. Grotesque elements if necessary.

↔︎ Eileen Zhang

Psychological depth, detached elegance, sharp observations of details, slightly cynical and melancholic tone.

↔︎ Yu Hua

Minimalist, simple short sentences, calm tone even in tense moments, focus on physical reality.

↔︎ Shui Qiang Cheng

Sharp emotional realism, power dynamics, control and vulnerability, psychological tension, direct language without embellishments.

↔︎ Fredrik Backman

Warm, observational narrator voice, humor and poignancy, attention to character quirks and hidden emotional depth behind stubborn facade.

❞︎ speech style
↔︎ cigarette confessions (J.D. Salinger)
★ Default

Description taken from Fawn's guide.

Universal. Literally. Characters swear, think like humans, panic, all super for RP in real world, students, hockey players etc. Makes them breathe (and eats prose a bit, but does it beautifully).

↔︎ sugar-coated razors (Flynn)

Description taken from Fawn's guide.

She is about dark romance in dialogues and thoughts, smiles hiding murder thoughts, elegant jabs. Very dark, very analytical and, as AI loves to say, predatory.

↔︎ warm cynicism (Pratchett)

Description taken from Fawn's guide.

Like Salinger in mod, just no swearing and not so explicit. Soft, warm and real. When you want character to be kind, but not an idiot. Will help old lady across road, but mentally pee himself laughing at her hat.

↔︎ earthsea wisdom (Le Guin)

Description taken from Fawn's guide.

For your characters to speak and think like fantasy, while you want prose from someone else. Just fantasy queen, adjusting to old communication norms etc.

↔︎ broken mirror (Chuck Palahniuk)

Description taken from Fawn's guide.

Very specific. VERY. Short, to the point, loops. Repetition is not a bug, it's a feature. He... just specific. Might fit someone. Cold, fight club and so on.

↔︎ velvet daggers (Wilde)

Description taken from Fawn's guide.

My god, yummy. Again. Read his style description and insert into dialogues. Jabs interestingly, lives sarcasm and is so... Well... Chic in primary meaning. Luxury max, luxe and list goes on. Prepare for your rude worker to suddenly speak like an aristocrat at a ball. Not a bug, it's Wilde.

🍎 Installation
▼
1

Download file

Go to the "Download" tab and get the latest JSON preset file.

2

Import to SillyTavern

Before installing, make sure API connection settings are set to "Chat Completion".

Then open the preset tab (leftmost button) and insert the preset as shown in screenshot.

Upon import a window will pop up. Press yes, Regexes will import.

3

Thinking Format

Go to AI Response Format section (third button from left), scroll down, expand "Thinking Template" and set everything exactly as in screenshot.

4

Choosing Prompts

Many prompts are "Templates" or "Replaceable".

Templates — completely empty toggles where you paste one or more (where possible) prompts. They are labeled: ❞︎ ✎ things (sample). Currently only this toggle has this system.

🔧 Functions
▼
1

◜Renette ooc◞

Disable/Enable OOC from Renette, logical?

↔︎ — this sign means you can select only one of the variants nearby, appears later too.

In disabled state you can still write instructions and requests like this:

(OOC: let's do this)

She might reply to this in next post, but won't write constantly.

2

◜base◞

All toggles in block must be on always (can replace their prompts with site variants, but not disable completely), but dont speak and speak for user cannot be enabled simultaneously.

language — you can just change two words in prompt (English, suppose), or take one of three variants from site.

Writing mode — Don't speak/speak for user, understandable overall.

↔︎ — this sign means you can select only one of the variants nearby, appears later too.

length — default: Medium in paragraphs. There's a myth that answer logic depends on it too. Claude listens better to length in words generally.

3

◜scribbling◞

rules and prose must be on always (can replace their prompts with site variants, but not disable completely), speech and focuses can be disabled.

rules — here you can change POV and time:

First person — 1st-person perspective

Second person — 2nd-person perspective

Past tense — past tense

Future tense — future tense

prose style — huge choice (not roleplay) and I update it (can add your author too). Answer and style heavily depend on prose style, try other options, their descriptions (and all prompt descriptions generally) are in Prompts tab on site.

(part from Fawn's guide)

Four dudes I don't advise mixing at all, if they are main authors:

— SALINGER. He will eat everything with his style. You can't take away dialogues/thoughts/humor from him. He lives by it.

— Jenny Lawson. Same as Salinger. RED WARNING do not mix.

— Terry Pratchett. Too warm to mix. Too much. You won't drive anything else into him. Except Lawson mod. They are quite a pair.

With HUGE exception: Wilde. He is too diva to mix. He will yield positions. But there are variants, just need to know how to pick.

speech style — Fawn's idea.

Basically character dialogue styles, read variants in prompt library section too.

(another part from her guide)

My favorite mixes:

cigarette confessions + ann rice. Love it die right now. Good for modern roleplays, trope doesn't matter. They are just good, give super base.

earthsea wisdom + ann rice. Solid fantasy, adjusts to lore and fears nothing.

velvet daggers + gillian flynn and vice versa. Cool couple for mafia-bosses living in luxury. Not luxury against cruelty background — luxury AS cruelty.

Focuses (magic) — three variants, can pick only one. How to say?.. Accentuates one aspect: world details, char dialogues, char thoughts. Work depends on model and writing mode. Can be disabled entirely.

4

◜branches◞

Prompts brazenly stolen from Fawn

All prompts here except maybe pace are "enhancements", meaning you can freely disable and enable them.

pace — relationship development pace. Default — slowburn, fast burn variant on site.

autonomous character — makes char more independent. Personality priority over plot.

world engine — task is to make world alive and force plot forward even if player is inactive.

npc autonomy — responsible for autonomy and depth of characters (NPC). Turns them from "cardboard decorations" into full story participants.

living world — responsible for presence effect and "living" world. Task is to make environment not just decoration but feel like real place.

speech enhance — responsible for speech authenticity and unique voice of each character. Task is to ensure heroes don't sound same, and their communication manner reflects past and character.

5

◜nsfw◞

Main instruction for NSFW and no horny (Rorati) — so characters don't get aroused without reason.

6

◜patches◞

bans — Choose one of three variants in prompts on site — Ru, En and Ukr language. If rolling in another language choose English version or remake prompt for your language.

anti-pronouns — fewer pronouns. "He", "She" etc., especially at sentence start. Indispensable for 2.5 about Gemini.

user silent turn — patch. Model doesn't complain if last reply in chat is from assistant.

7

◜tweaks◞

colored dialogues — think it's clear.

colored emphasis — colors some accent words, works poorly on dumb models.

html prompt — HTML blocks throughout plot.

✎ things (sample) — can shove bunch of stuff from site here, pick several and shove.

html theme — can enable to choose theme for HTML. Default is black, but there is light variant in prompts tab.

tweaks language — asks to follow language in HTML text etc. (content in tweaks) specified in .

8

◜COT◞

Thinking Logic. Segmented, can disable some parts so model thinks faster, but will consider and check less.

Can disable all toggles from to if want to turn it off.

9

◜prefill◞

Choose depending on model.

For DeepSeek disable entirely (same as thinking logic).
